Workshop
The 1st International Workshop on
Agentic Multimodal Intelligence
Rio de Janeiro, Brazil • 10–11 November 2026

Recent advances in multimodal foundation models have significantly reshaped artificial intelligence by enabling unified perception, understanding, and generation across text, images, videos, audio, and other modalities. However, the next frontier of intelligence lies in agentic AI—systems that can autonomously reason, plan, act, and adapt over time in interactive environments. This shift marks a fundamental transition from passive multimodal models to Agentic Multimodal Intelligence (AMI). This workshop brings together researchers across multimedia, vision, NLP, robotics, and HCI to advance models, data, benchmarks, and evaluation for next-generation intelligent agents.

Call for Papers

We invite submissions of original research contributions related to, but not limited to, the following topics:

  1. Agentic Multimodal Intelligence & Autonomous Decision-Making
  2. Multimodal Reasoning, Planning, and Control
  3. Learning from Interaction and Human Feedback
  4. Data, Benchmarks, and Evaluation for Multimodal Agents
  5. Trustworthy and Responsible Multimodal Agents

Papers follow the ACM MM 2026 format (4–8 pages + up to 2 reference pages), are reviewed double-blind, and will be archived in the ACM Digital Library. Submit via OpenReview.
