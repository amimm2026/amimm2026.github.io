Overview

About the Workshop

Recent advances in multimodal foundation models—including large vision-language models and multimodal generative models—have significantly reshaped artificial intelligence by enabling unified perception, understanding, and generation across text, images, videos, audio, and other sensory modalities.

Beyond perception and generation, a growing body of research indicates that the next frontier of intelligence lies in agentic AI. Increasingly, multimodal foundation models are being deployed as agents: systems that can autonomously reason, plan, act, and adapt over time in interactive environments. This shift marks a fundamental transition from passive multimodal models to Agentic Multimodal Intelligence.

This workshop brings together researchers and practitioners across multimedia, vision, natural language processing, robotics, and human-computer interaction to advance agentic intelligence—fostering interdisciplinary discussion on models, data, benchmarks, and evaluation for next-generation intelligent agents.

Key Research Questions

  • How can multimodal foundation models be endowed with agency, enabling autonomous decision-making rather than passive response generation?
  • How can agents effectively integrate perception, reasoning, memory, and action across heterogeneous modalities?
  • How can multimodal agents perform long-horizon planning and reasoning in dynamic and partially observable environments?
  • How can agents learn from interaction, feedback, and experience, rather than relying solely on static supervision?
  • How should we evaluate agentic multimodal intelligence, particularly in terms of robustness, safety, and long-term behavior?
Submissions

Call for Papers

We invite submissions of original research contributions related to, but not limited to, the following topics:

Agentic Multimodal Intelligence

  • Multimodal agents and autonomous decision-making
  • Vision-Language-Action models
  • Tool-using and environment-interacting agents
  • Generative agents for decision-making and planning
  • Long-horizon task planning with multimodal inputs
  • Memory, state representation, and world models for agents

Multimodal Reasoning, Planning, and Control

  • Multimodal reasoning over time and events
  • Hierarchical planning and skill composition
  • Multimodal reinforcement learning and decision-making
  • Embodied and simulated interactive environments

Learning from Interaction and Feedback

  • Online, continual, and lifelong learning for multimodal agents
  • Learning from human feedback and preferences
  • Self-reflection, self-improvement, and adaptation
  • Human-in-the-loop multimodal agent systems
  • Generative modeling of experience and trajectories

Data, Benchmarks, and Evaluation

  • Benchmarks for agentic multimodal intelligence
  • Interactive datasets and simulation environments
  • Long-horizon and multi-step evaluation protocols
  • Evaluation of agents' behaviors and long-term outcomes
  • Failure analysis and diagnostic evaluation

Trustworthy and Responsible Multimodal Agents

  • Safety, robustness, and alignment in agentic systems
  • Interpretability and transparency of agent decisions
  • Bias, fairness, and ethical considerations
  • Reliability and controllability of generative agent behaviors
  • Responsible deployment of agents in the real world

Invited Talks

Keynote Speakers

HH

Helen Huang

The University of Queensland, Australia

"Generative Multimodal Agents for Long-horizon Reasoning"

Professor and ARC Future Fellow at the School of ITEE, The University of Queensland. Her research interests include multimedia indexing and search, and social data analysis. She has published 200+ papers and serves as Associate Editor of VLDB Journal and ACM TOIS.

JL

Jiebo Luo

The University of Rochester, USA

"Vision-Language-Action Models for Agentic Intelligence"

Professor at the University of Rochester. His research spans computer vision, NLP, machine learning, and data mining. He has authored nearly 600 technical papers and 90+ U.S. patents. Fellow of ACM, AAAI, IEEE, SPIE, and IAPR.

JC

Jianfei Cai

Monash University, Australia

"World Models and Multimodal Planning for Interactive Agents"

Professor at the Faculty of IT, Monash University. His research interests include computer vision, multimedia, and visual computing. He has published 300+ technical papers and is General Chair for ACM Multimedia 2024. Fellow of IEEE.

NS

Nicu Sebe

The University of Trento, Italy

"Multimodal Perception and Reasoning in Open World"

Professor at the University of Trento, leading research in multimedia information retrieval and human behavior understanding. General Co-Chair of ACM Multimedia 2013, and Program Chair of ACM Multimedia 2007, 2011, ECCV 2016, and ICCV 2017.
